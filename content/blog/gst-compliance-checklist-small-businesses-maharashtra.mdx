GST compliance is not a one-time activity — it is an ongoing calendar of returns, payments, and reconciliations. For small businesses in Pune and across Maharashtra, missing even a single deadline can cascade into interest charges, late fees, and ITC blockage.

This checklist is designed to be printed and pinned to your office wall.

---

## Know Your Return Category First

Your compliance obligations depend on your annual aggregate turnover:

| Turnover | Return Type | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Up to ₹1.5 crore (opted-in) | GSTR-1 (Quarterly) + GSTR-3B (Quarterly) | **QRMP Scheme** |
| Up to ₹5 crore | GSTR-1 + GSTR-3B | Monthly |
| Above ₹5 crore | GSTR-1 + GSTR-3B + GSTR-9 | Monthly + Annual |
| Composition dealers | GSTR-4 | Annual (with quarterly CMP-08) |

---

## Monthly Compliance Calendar

### By 11th of Every Month
- **GSTR-1** — Outward supply details (invoices issued to customers)
  - Must include all B2B invoices, B2C summary, debit/credit notes, and export invoices
  - QRMP filers: submit Invoice Furnishing Facility (IFF) for B2B only

### By 13th of Every Month
- **GSTR-2B auto-populates** — Review your auto-drafted ITC statement
  - Cross-check with your purchase register and vendor GSTR-1 filings
  - Investigate mismatches before claiming ITC

### By 20th of Every Month
- **GSTR-3B** — Summary return + tax payment
  - Report outward supplies, ITC claimed, and net tax payable
  - **Pay tax by 20th** — even a one-day delay attracts 18% p.a. interest on the tax due

---

## Quarterly Compliance (QRMP Scheme)

If you are on QRMP (turnover ≤ ₹1.5 crore):

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| Month 1 of quarter | Pay tax via PMT-06 challan by 25th |
| Month 2 of quarter | Pay tax via PMT-06 challan by 25th |
| Quarter end month | File GSTR-3B by 22nd (Category I states) or 24th (Category II states) |

> **Maharashtra is a Category I state** — QRMP filers must file GSTR-3B by the **22nd** of the month following the quarter.

---

## Annual Compliance

### GSTR-9 — Annual Return
- **Who must file**: All regular taxpayers (mandatory above ₹2 crore; voluntary for ₹2 crore and below)
- **Due date**: December 31 of the next financial year (e.g., for FY 2023-24, due by 31 Dec 2024)
- **What it covers**: Consolidated annual data of supplies, purchases, ITC, and tax paid

### GSTR-9C — Self-Certified Reconciliation
- **Who must file**: Taxpayers with turnover above ₹5 crore
- **Filed along with GSTR-9**
- Reconciles GSTR-9 figures with audited financial statements

## ITC Reconciliation — The Most Critical Task

Input Tax Credit (ITC) claimed incorrectly is a common reason for GST notices. Every month:

✅ Download GSTR-2B  
✅ Compare with your purchase register  
✅ Identify invoices where supplier has not filed GSTR-1  
✅ Follow up with supplier or reverse the ITC claim  
✅ Never claim ITC on ineligible items (Section 17(5) — food, club membership, motor vehicles, etc.)

**Rule 36(4)**: You can only claim ITC up to the amount appearing in GSTR-2B. Excess claims are now automatically flagged.

## Common Penalties to Avoid

| Violation | Penalty / Consequence |
|---|---|
| Late GSTR-1 | ₹50/day (₹20/day for Nil return), max ₹10,000 |
| Late GSTR-3B | ₹50/day (₹20/day for Nil), max ₹10,000 + 18% p.a. interest on tax |
| Wrong ITC claim | Recovery + 100% penalty (up to 200% in fraud cases) |
| Non-registration despite liability | 100% of tax due or ₹10,000, whichever is higher |
| Late GSTR-9 | ₹200/day, max 0.25% of turnover |

## E-Invoice Applicability (Mandatory in Maharashtra)

If your aggregate turnover exceeds **₹5 crore** in any preceding financial year, e-invoicing is mandatory for all B2B and B2G supplies. 

Key points:
- Generate IRN (Invoice Reference Number) from the IRP portal **before** sharing the invoice with the buyer
- QR code must be embedded in the invoice
- E-invoices auto-populate GSTR-1 — manual entry becomes redundant

## Practical Tips for Small Business Owners in Pune

1. **Reconcile books weekly**, not at the end of the month. Catching mismatches early is far less painful.
2. **Set calendar alerts** on the 9th and 18th of every month as internal deadlines — gives you buffer before the actual due date.
3. **Maintain a vendor compliance tracker** — if a vendor consistently fails to file GSTR-1, your ITC is at risk.
4. **Never ignore GST notices** — even an automated DRC-01 for ITC mismatch must be replied to within the specified time.
5. **E-way bills and GST are linked** — wrong GSTIN on e-way bill leads to automatic discrepancy flags.
