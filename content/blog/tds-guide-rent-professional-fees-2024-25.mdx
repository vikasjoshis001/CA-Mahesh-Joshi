Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is one of the most common sources of penalties for small businesses, freelancers, and professionals. The rules are spread across multiple sections of the Income Tax Act, and the thresholds change periodically.

This guide covers the TDS provisions that apply to the most common business payments — so you know exactly when to deduct, at what rate, and by when to deposit.

---

## Why TDS Matters More Than You Think

If you were **required to deduct TDS** but didn't:

1. **The entire expense becomes disallowed** under Section 40(a)(ia) — meaning you lose the tax deduction on that payment
2. **Interest**: 1% per month for non-deduction, 1.5% per month for deduction but non-deposit
3. **Penalty**: Up to 100% of TDS amount under Section 271C
4. **Prosecution**: In serious cases under Section 276B

For a business in the 30% tax bracket paying ₹12 lakh annual rent without deducting TDS — the disallowance alone costs ₹3.6 lakh in extra tax. The TDS itself would have been only ₹24,000. The maths is stark.

## The Most Important TDS Sections for Businesses

### Section 194C — Payments to Contractors

**Applicable when**: You make payment to an Indian resident for carrying out any work (including supply of labour) or for a contract.

Examples: Printing & advertising, transportation, catering, civil work, event management.

| Condition | Rate |
|---|---|
| Payment to individual/HUF | **1%** |
| Payment to others (company, firm, AOP) | **2%** |

**Threshold**: 
- Single payment: ₹30,000 or more, OR
- Aggregate payments to the same contractor in FY: ₹1,00,000 or more

> Deduct TDS **at the time of credit or payment, whichever is earlier**.

---

### Section 194J — Professional or Technical Fees

**Applicable when**: You pay professional fees, technical service fees, royalty, or director's fees.

Examples: Fees to lawyers, doctors, CAs, engineers, consultants, software developers.

| Nature of Payment | Rate |
|---|---|
| Technical services (call centre, data processing, etc.) | **2%** |
| Professional services (legal, medical, CA, engineering) | **10%** |
| Royalty | **10%** |
| Director's fees (not salary) | **10%** |

**Threshold**: ₹30,000 per payee per FY (no threshold for director fees).

---

### Section 194I — Rent

**Applicable when**: You pay rent for land, building, plant, machinery, furniture, or fittings.

| Type of Rent | Rate |
|---|---|
| Plant, machinery, equipment | **2%** |
| Land, building, furniture, fittings | **10%** |

**Threshold**: ₹2,40,000 per year per landlord (₹20,000/month and above in effect).

**Common confusion**: If you pay ₹18,000/month rent (₹2,16,000/year) — NO TDS required.  
At ₹21,000/month (₹2,52,000/year) — TDS at 10% applies on entire amount.

---

### Section 194IB — Rent Paid by Individuals/HUF (Not Subject to Tax Audit)

This section applies to **individuals and HUFs who are not required to get a tax audit** (i.e., income below the threshold) but pay rent exceeding **₹50,000 per month**.

Rate: **5%**  
Deduct only once a year — in the last month of tenancy or last month of FY, whichever is earlier.  
Use **Form 26QC** to deposit (not regular challan 281).

---

### Section 194A — Interest Other Than on Securities

**Applicable when**: You pay interest on unsecured loans or deposits.

Rate: **10%**  
Threshold: ₹40,000 per year (₹50,000 for senior citizens) from banks/NBFCs; ₹5,000 for others.

---

### Section 194H — Commission and Brokerage

Rate: **5%**  
Threshold: ₹15,000 per year.

## TDS Deposit Deadlines

Once deducted, TDS must be deposited to the government by:

| Deductor | Month | Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| All deductors (except government) | April–February | 7th of next month |
| All deductors (except government) | March | 30th April |
| Government deductors | Same day if paid without challan |

**One-day delay = 1.5% interest per month on the TDS amount.**

---

## TDS Returns — Quarterly Filing

Every deductor must file quarterly TDS returns:

| Form | Purpose | Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| **Form 26Q** | All non-salary TDS (194C, 194J, 194I, etc.) | 31st July / 31st Oct / 31st Jan / 31st May |
| **Form 24Q** | Salary TDS | Same as above |
| **Form 27Q** | TDS on payments to non-residents | Same as above |

Late filing penalty: ₹200 per day under Section 234E.

---

## Lower or Nil TDS Certificate

If your income has deductions/exemptions and TDS would result in a refund, you can apply for a **lower deduction certificate under Section 197** from your jurisdictional Assessing Officer. Present this certificate to the party deducting TDS.

---

## Practical TDS Checklist for Small Businesses in Pune

✅ Maintain a register of all vendor payments with PAN, amount, and TDS status  
✅ Collect PAN of all vendors at the time of engagement (higher TDS at 20% if no PAN)  
✅ Set up separate challan 281 payment in net banking for TDS deposits  
✅ File 26Q quarterly — don't let the ₹200/day penalty accumulate  
✅ Issue TDS certificates (Form 16A) to vendors within 15 days of filing the quarterly return  
✅ Download Form 26AS regularly — reconcile with what you have deducted
