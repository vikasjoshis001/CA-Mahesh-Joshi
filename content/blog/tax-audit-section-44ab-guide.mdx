The income tax audit under **Section 44AB** is one of the most misunderstood compliance requirements for businesses and professionals in India. Many small business owners believe an audit is only for large corporations — and end up with penalty notices for non-compliance.

Let me simplify this completely.

---

## What is a Tax Audit?

A tax audit under Section 44AB is not the same as a statutory audit under the Companies Act. It is an examination of your books of accounts by a **practising Chartered Accountant**, who then certifies their findings in **Form 3CA/3CB** and submits a detailed statement in **Form 3CD**.

The purpose is to ensure that:
- Your books of accounts are maintained correctly
- Income is correctly computed as per the Income Tax Act
- Required disclosures are made to the tax department

---

## Who is Required to Get a Tax Audit?

### Businesses (Section 44AB(a))

If your **total sales, turnover, or gross receipts** exceed **₹1 crore** in any financial year, a tax audit is mandatory.

**Exception — higher threshold of ₹10 crore**: If your cash receipts and cash payments each do not exceed 5% of total receipts and payments respectively (i.e., at least 95% transactions are digital), the limit is raised to **₹10 crore**.

Most Pune-based businesses that have moved to UPI/banking transactions benefit from this higher limit.

### Professionals (Section 44AB(b))

If your **gross receipts** exceed **₹50 lakh** in any financial year, a tax audit is mandatory.

*Professionals include doctors, lawyers, engineers, architects, CAs, company secretaries, interior decorators, and technical consultants as defined under Section 44AA.*

### Presumptive Taxation Schemes — When Audit Triggers

This is where many businesses get caught off-guard.

**Section 44AD (Presumptive business income)**:
- You are eligible if turnover ≤ ₹3 crore (with 95%+ digital transactions) or ₹2 crore otherwise
- You declare income at **8% of turnover** (6% for digital receipts) — no books required
- **BUT**: If you opt out of 44AD (declare income lower than 8%), you **must** get a tax audit — even if turnover is only ₹50 lakh

**Section 44ADA (Presumptive professional income)**:
- Professionals can declare **50% of gross receipts** as income
- If you opt out and declare lower — **tax audit is mandatory** regardless of amount

## The Due Date

The tax audit report (Form 3CA/3CB + 3CD) must be submitted by **30th September** of the assessment year (i.e., by 30 Sep 2024 for FY 2023-24).

This date is the same as the ITR due date for audit cases — you need the audit report before you can file your return.

> **Note**: CBDT sometimes extends this deadline via circulars. Always check the latest notifications, especially during October.

---

## What Documents Does Your CA Need for the Audit?

Provide these to your CA well before September to avoid last-minute rush:

**Books of Accounts**
- Cash book and bank book
- Sales and purchase ledgers
- Journal and ledger with all entries
- Stock register (for trading/manufacturing businesses)

**Supporting Documents**
- All bank statements for the FY
- TDS certificates (Form 16A from customers who deducted TDS)
- Copies of all GST returns filed (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9)
- Investment details, loan statements, fixed asset register
- Contracts/agreements for major transactions

**Tax Documents**
- Previous year's audited accounts and ITR
- All advance tax and self-assessment tax challans
- 26AS and AIS statements

---

## What Does Form 3CD Contain?

Form 3CD has **44 clauses** covering:

- Nature of business and method of accounting
- Changes in accounting method from previous year
- Valuation of closing stock
- Payments to specified persons (related parties, partners)
- TDS compliance — whether TDS was deducted and deposited on time
- Loans and advances taken/given in cash above ₹20,000
- Unreported income from foreign sources
- Speculation business transactions
- Details of payments to MSME suppliers beyond 45 days (Section 43B(h) — new from FY 2023-24)

The audit is thorough. Your CA is signing a professional certificate — accuracy is non-negotiable.

---

## Penalty for Not Getting a Tax Audit

If your turnover exceeds the prescribed limit and you do not get the audit done or do not file Form 3CA/3CD by the due date:

**Penalty under Section 271B**: 0.5% of total sales/turnover/gross receipts, **maximum ₹1,50,000**.

However, the penalty can be waived if you demonstrate a "reasonable cause" for non-compliance (illness, natural disaster, etc.). Courts have also held that genuine ignorance of the law is not a reasonable cause.

---

## Section 43B(h) — New Compliance from FY 2023-24

Budget 2023 introduced a significant new clause. If you are a **buyer from an MSME-registered supplier**, and you have not paid within:
- 15 days (if no written agreement), or
- 45 days (if there is a written agreement as per MSMED Act)

Then the **expense is not deductible** in FY 2023-24. It gets deducted only in the year of actual payment.

Your CA will now specifically verify and report MSME payment status in Form 3CD. Businesses with many small vendors must maintain a proper MSME status database.
